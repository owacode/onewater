Meet the Chief Resilience Officers, who are top-level governmental advisors with expertise in maximizing innovation while minimizing the impact of unforeseen events.

Who is CRO ?

A Chief Resilience Officer acts as a municipality’s point person for resilience building, helping to coordinate resilience efforts across departments and other agencies. Many CROs offer scalable knowledge and solutions that can be applied to the issues of other municipalities. The CROs who have joined OneWater are acutely aware that they have the power to help address the world’s water issues. Share your accomplishments. Your goals. Your vision. Join the CROs.

What CROs have to say about OneWater

Miami-Dade County’s water future is at a crossroads. On the one hand, we are experiencing challenges or shocks and stresses like we have never seen before, from sea level rise, saltwater intrusion, increasing and more intense hurricanes and sunny day flooding to aging infrastructure. On the other, the stewards of water—our utility managers, public officials, agricultural community, non-profits, businesses, manufacturers, community leaders, recreational users, and more—are shaping and creating transformative solutions. We have come a long way to get here, and as one of the largest counties in the nation there is much at stake. The innovative and holistic approach of the One Water Academy with its mission to advance knowledge sharing, learning, and capacity building in the water sector will help propel us forward to a more Resilient305.

- James Murley Chief
Resilience Officer
Miami-Dade County
